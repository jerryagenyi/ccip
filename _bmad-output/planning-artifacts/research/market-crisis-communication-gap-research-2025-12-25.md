## Research Overview

This market research investigates the competitive landscape and market gaps for crisis communication management platforms, with specific focus on public health contexts in low-resource settings. The research validates assumptions behind CCIP (Crisis Communication Intelligence Platform) and tests the hypothesis that existing solutions fail to address three critical needs: (1) semiotic intelligence for predicting message effectiveness, (2) cross-organizational learning through federated insights, and (3) offline-first usability for non-tech-savvy field workers.

**Research Questions:**
1. What proprietary solutions exist for crisis/risk communication management in African contexts?
2. Is there a publicly available non-proprietary alternative for coordinated risk communication at scale in Africa?
3. Why have existing solutions not addressed fragmentation, silos, and lack of insight problems?
4. What academic research exists on semiotic analysis in health communication?
5. What can be learned from adjacent domains (disaster response, humanitarian aid, public health surveillance)?
6. Do crisis communication practitioners confirm the three-gap thesis?

**Methodology:** Web-based research with source verification, competitive analysis, and academic literature review.
